🫧 Salivary CAR – Cortisol & Cortisone

0 flagged ▼
Salivary cortisol and cortisone reflect the Cortisol Awakening Response (CAR) and circadian HPA axis rhythm. Saliva measures bioavailable (unbound) hormone. Cortisone in saliva reflects local 11β-HSD2 conversion and closely mirrors serum free cortisol. The Insomnia sample is collected if the patient wakes at night.
Salivary Free Cortisol
Saliva Cortisol – Waking
Baseline upon waking; initiates CAR; reflects initial adrenal output
Saliva Cortisol – W+30 min
Peak CAR; should rise 50–200% above waking; blunted = HPA suppression
Saliva Cortisol – W+60 min
Should begin declining from W+30 peak; sustained high = CAR dysregulation
Saliva Cortisol – Evening
Should be low; elevated = inverted rhythm, chronic stress, or poor sleep
Saliva Cortisol – Night
Should be very low; elevated = nocturnal HPA activation, poor sleep architecture
Saliva Cortisol – Insomnia
Collected upon nocturnal waking; elevated confirms stress-driven insomnia
Saliva Cortisol – Total
Cumulative daily cortisol exposure via saliva; ref 11.0–24.0 ng/mL
Salivary Free Cortisone
Saliva Cortisone – Waking
Inactive cortisol metabolite (11β-HSD2 in salivary glands); mirrors serum free cortisol
Saliva Cortisone – W+30 min
Waking + 30 min; reflects 11β-HSD2 activity at CAR peak
Saliva Cortisone – W+60 min
Should begin declining; sustained elevation = prolonged morning HPA activation
Saliva Cortisone – Evening
Ref 3.2–6.8 ng/mL; elevated = prolonged stress or abnormal 11β-HSD2 activity
Saliva Cortisone – Night
Ref 1.3–3.4 ng/mL; elevated = nocturnal HPA hyperactivity; linked to insomnia and metabolic risk
Saliva Cortisone – Insomnia
Ref 1.3–3.4 ng/mL; confirms nocturnal HPA activation; reflects disrupted circadian cortisone regulation
Saliva Cortisone – Total
Cumulative daily cortisone exposure; ref 34.0–65.0 ng/mL; >65 = chronic HPA activation
